WHY THIS EXISTS

Blotter exists because I needed it. After a rough quarter trading micro futures, I wanted to see my days honestly and write down what I kept repeating — without paying $40 a month or handing my trading history to someone else’s server. So it’s simple on purpose: a calendar, a trade log, a journal. Free, in your browser, yours.

It’s early, and I’m building it in the open — send feedback and help make it better.

FAQ
Is Blotter really free?+

Yes — Blotter is free while in beta. No account, no card, no trial timer.

Where does my trading data go?+

Nowhere. Parsing happens in your browser and results are stored on your device. There’s no server to send them to — we never see a single trade.

Which brokers are supported?+

Interactive Brokers today — both the one-click Transaction History CSV and Flex Query exports. More brokers will follow based on what early users ask for.

How does auto-refresh work — do you ever see my data?+

Auto-refresh uses IBKR’s Flex Web Service: you paste a query ID and a read-only reporting token, stored on your device. IBKR blocks direct browser connections, so the request passes through an open-source, stateless relay that keeps nothing — no logs, no storage, no accounts. Parsing and storage still happen in your browser. Blotter refreshes when you open it and every few minutes while it stays open, so new trades appear within minutes of the fill.

What instruments does it handle?+

Futures, options and stocks. Multi-currency accounts are converted to your base currency, and totals are checked against your statement’s cash balances.

Can I move to a new computer or browser?+

Yes — Export backup produces one file with your journal, tags, setups and imported data. Restore it on the new machine and you’re exactly where you left off.
